Introduction to isro.

The Indian Space Research Organisation[a] (ISRO; /ˈɪsroʊ/) is the national space agency of India, headquartered in Bangalore, which operates under the Department of Space (DOS) and is responsible for conducting space research, space exploration, and the development of technologies necessary for space-based applications.

isro logo.

The Indian space programme started with INCOSPAR which was established in February 1962. It was later superseded by ISRO on 15th August 1969, to institutionalize space research in India. In 1972, the Government of India set up a Space Commission and brought ISRO under the DOS. India's first satellite Aryabhata, which had been built and integrated by ISRO, was launched in 1975 by the Interkosmos (Soviet Union). In 1980, ISRO launched the RS-1 satellite onboard SLV, its first launch vehicle making India the seventh country which was capable of undertaking orbital launches. SLV was subsequently followed by the development of many launch vehicles, rocket engines, satellite systems and networks enabling the agency to launch hundreds of domestic and foreign satellites and conduct various deep space missions for space exploration. India became one of the few countries which had sent a spacecraft to the Moon in 2008 when its Chandrayaan probe entered the lunar orbit. The data from the probe eventually helped in confirming the existence of water on the lunar surface. ISRO launched the MOM spacecraft in 2013, which reached Mars on 24 September 2014, making India the first Asian nation, the fourth nation overall in the world, and also the first nation to have achieved this milestone on the first try. ISRO launched a record number of 104 satellites in a PSLV, from SDSC on 15th February 2017, which beat the previous Russian record of launching 37 satellites in one rocket in 2014. A significant milestone in ISRO's history was achieved with the launch of GSAT-14 in a GSLV MK II having an Indian-made CE-7.5 cryogenic engine in 2014. With the success of this mission , ISRO became one of the six countries that have mastered this technology, demonstrated full launch capabilities and have sent extraterrestrial missions to other planets. On 5th June 2017, ISRO launched LVM3 carrying GSAT-19 communications satellite. The mission was a success, making LVM3 the heaviest rocket to be launched and the GSAT-19 satellite, the heaviest Indian payload to be launched into space till then.

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam BR (/ˈɑːbdəl kəˈlɑːm/ (listen); 15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering.
